Delicious Chicken Biryani

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 3-4 green cardamom pods
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon sticks
  • Salt to taste

For the Chicken marinade

  • 500 grams chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste

Cooking Terms

  • Marination: The process of soaking meat, seafood, or vegetables in a mixture of spices, yogurt.
  • Dum: A slow cooking technique where the biryani is cooked on a low flame, often with the lid sealed with dough, to allow the flavors to meld and the rice to absorb the essence of the spices.
  • Bhoona: The method of sautéing spices and ingredients until they release their oils and become aromatic, forming the base of the biryani masala.
  • Deglazing: Adding liquid to a hot pan to loosen and dissolve the browned bits of food stuck to the bottom, creating a rich and flavorful base for the biryani.
  • Layering: The process of alternating layers of partially cooked rice and marinated meat or vegetables in a pot before the final cooking stage.

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Rice

1. Wash the Basmati rice thoroughly under running water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain.
2. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the bay leaf, cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, and salt.
3. Add the soaked and drained rice to the boiling water. Cook until the rice is 70-80% done. Drain the rice and set aside.

Step 2: Marinating the Chicken

In a large bowl, mix together the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well-coated.
Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for best results.

Step 3: Cooking the Chicken

1. Heat the ghee or oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat.
2. Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown. Remove half of the fried onions and set aside for garnish.
3. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies to the pot and sauté for another 2-3 minutes.
4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and oil starts to separate.
5. Add the marinated chicken and cook until the chicken is almost cooked through. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala powder. Stir well to combine.
6. Add chopped coriander and mint leaves. Mix well.

Step 4: Layering the Biryani

1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) if you plan to use the oven for the final cooking step.
2. In the same pot with the cooked chicken, spread half of the par-cooked rice over the chicken layer.
3. Drizzle half of the saffron milk over the rice (if using). Repeat with the remaining rice and saffron milk.
4. Sprinkle the reserved fried onions on top. You can also add more fresh coriander and mint leaves.
5. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or aluminum foil to seal in the steam.

Step 5: Cooking the Biryani (Dum Cooking)

If using the stovetop, place a tawa (griddle) under the pot to prevent direct heat. Cook on low heat for 25-30 minutes.
If using the oven, place the sealed pot in the preheated oven and cook for 25-30 minutes.
After the cooking time, turn off the heat and let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before opening the lid.

Step 6: Serving the Biryani

Gently fluff up the rice with a fork, mixing the layers.
Serve hot, garnished with lemon slices and additional fresh coriander leaves, if desired.

Enjoy your delicious, homemade chicken biryani!
